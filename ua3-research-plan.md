(aol.com West Lafayette sectionals) — Treat "very often" as structurally plausible but BEST CONFIRMED IN INTERVIEWS with real junior players.
  - **Interpretation (Joshua, 2026-06-15):** Structure supports the target-customer claim. The key assumption — repeat opponents within a section — stays UNCONFIRMED and is the top thing to verify in interviews. Target-customer clause stays drafted-unconfirmed.

## Primary competitive alternative

- **Current claim:** Relying on memory and asking teammates/coaches; some keep scattered paper notes. None of the alternatives are apps.
- **Questions:**
  - What tennis apps already exist for logging matches / tracking stats / scouting opponents (e.g., SwingVision, TennisKeeper, Tennis Tracker, others)? What do they actually do?
  - Do any of them do opponent-specific scouting or "how to beat this player" tips?
  - What do reviews of these apps complain about (app store reviews)?
- **Sources:** App Store / Google Play (app descriptions + reviews), competitor websites, Reddit/forum threads recommending tennis apps.
- **Enough:** Stop once I've identified the 4–6 most relevant existing apps and read enough reviews to know their common complaints and whether any does opponent scouting.
- **Expected findings:** Joshua predicts some apps log matches / track stats but none give "how to beat them" tips. His biggest worry: finding that lots of apps already do exactly this.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-15, searched "best tennis apps log matches track stats opponents." Found: **TennisKeeper** logs match scores + stats and keeps a win/loss record AND head-to-head statistics vs specific opponents; **SwingVision** does AI video stats / auto scoring; Tennistracker, Zepp Tennis also track stats. (tennisgearguide.co.uk; swing.vision) — So H2H records already exist in a shipping app.
  - 2026-06-15, searched "tennis app scout opponent how to beat strengths weaknesses." Found: **Opponote** — "the simplest way to log matches and capture strategies" for tennis and other head-to-head sports: record scores, tag opponents by skill tier / dominant hand, Elo ratings, win-probability, customizable scouting note cards, on-device. This is very close to MatchBook's journal + opponent-scouting concept. (apps.apple.com/us/app/opponote)
  - 2026-06-15, follow-up search on Opponote: confirmed it is a **manual** match-logging + opponent-notes app — YOU jot the tactics; it does not auto-generate "how to beat them" advice. (apps.apple.com/us/app/opponote)
  - 2026-06-15, AI apps found: **SwingVision, TennisAI, OnCourtAI** all give AI coaching feedback on YOUR strokes/technique from video — not opponent-specific tactical plans, not "what to practice" from match logs. **Tennis Notes** is another manual match tracker. (apps.apple.com; oncourtai.co.uk)
  - 2026-06-15, fetched Opponote App Store page: **Free**, iPhone-only, first released July 2025, currently v1.5, only 2 ratings, 2.2 MB, no accounts/cloud, no AI. Indie (developer Roque Jean). "You might also like" row shows many similar small match-trackers (Deucey, Tennis Alley, "Tennis Journal Tennis Notebook," TennisData2, Tennisphere). (apps.apple.com/us/app/opponote) — Signal: many people are building manual match-loggers (demand is real), the field is fragmented among tiny low-traction apps, and none has an AI tactical/practice layer.
  - **Net:** logging, H2H, and manual opponent notes are all solved (TennisKeeper, Opponote). AI on your own strokes is solved (SwingVision/TennisAI/OnCourtAI). The gap nobody fills: AI that reads your logged matches and outputs (1) a tactical plan to beat a SPECIFIC opponent's style, and (2) automatic "what to practice" recommendations.
  - **Interpretation (Joshua, 2026-06-15):** Prediction was close but Opponote is nearer than expected — it does manual opponent notes, just not AI tips. Decision: NARROW the differentiation to the AI layer (auto tactical plan + auto practice tips); widen the competitive-alternative clause to include manual logging apps. The crowded logging field actually validates demand; the AI tips are the open gap and the main risk to prove out.
