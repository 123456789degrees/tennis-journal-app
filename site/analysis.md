## What this step was

With the positioning statement holding up, the next step went one layer deeper: who besides the player has a stake in this product, what constraints (like privacy and COPPA) that creates, and — most importantly — what specific jobs the app needs to do for someone, described as concrete scenarios rather than abstract features.

## Result

<div class="results-box">
<h3>Stakeholders</h3>
<p><strong>Parents</strong> turned out to be the key stakeholder beyond the player: they're the ones pushing the journaling habit in the first place, and they're both the source of the pain (nagging to journal) and the most likely adoption lever (the parent who encourages or pays for the app). Coaches were considered but aren't central — most players in this world don't loop a coach into match prep.</p>
</div>

<div class="results-box">
<h3>Privacy &amp; compliance</h3>
<p>MatchMind is <strong>private and personal by design</strong> — no public or shared database of players or opponents; everything is visible only to the player who logged it. Because the under-13 age bracket falls within junior competitive tennis, a <strong>parent is involved in the account for under-13 users</strong>, satisfying COPPA naturally rather than as an afterthought.</p>
</div>

<div class="results-box">
<h3>The three jobs</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Capture a match without the effort</strong> — log what happened in seconds, right when a player is drained or upset, without sitting down to write a journal entry.</li>
<li><strong>Recall an opponent before a rematch</strong> — instantly see how someone plays and what worked last time, even if it was a year ago.</li>
<li><strong>Know what to practice</strong> (the bonus) — automatically surface a recurring weakness from recent matches and suggest a drill, without the player having to go looking for it.</li>
</ol>
<p>Each job was written as a full scenario — a specific moment, something going wrong, and how the product recovers — and all three were confirmed to deliver the positioning statement's key benefit when walked through the wireframes. See <a href="{{ '/specification-wireframes.html' | relative_url }}">Specification &amp; Wireframes</a>.</p>
</div>
