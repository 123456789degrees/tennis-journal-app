## What this step was

A positioning statement forces you to answer seven questions about a product before you build it: who it's for, what problem it solves, what category it's in, what name it goes by, what the core benefit is, what people do today instead, and what makes it actually different from that. It started as a first draft based on belief (Joshua's own experience as a competitive junior player), then got revised across several passes as research and real interviews came in.

The biggest revision happened after interviews: the original idea leaned on AI tactical tips ("know how to beat them") as the main draw. Real players cared much less about that than expected — what they actually hated was the effort of manual journaling. The whole positioning pivoted around that finding.

## Result

<blockquote>
<p>For <strong>junior competitive tennis players who compete in USTA tournaments (roughly ages 12–18, possibly college players) and face the same opponents repeatedly over a season</strong>,</p>
<p>who <strong>are pushed to keep a tennis journal but hate doing it by hand, and want to remember opponents and what worked — even a year later — without the effort</strong>,</p>
<p><strong>MatchMind</strong> is a <strong>tennis match journal and opponent-scouting app</strong> — you log past matches, then look up any opponent to see their playstyle and get tips on how to beat them —</p>
<p>that <strong>captures your matches in seconds and hands it all back the moment you need it — so you keep a journal worth having without the chore</strong>.</p>
<p>Unlike <strong>relying on memory, asking teammates or coaches, or manual logging apps where you write and interpret your own opponent notes (like Opponote)</strong>,</p>
<p>MatchMind <strong>makes logging so fast and automatic that you actually keep it up, then instantly gives back your full head-to-head history and what worked against this opponent — with AI tips on how to beat them and what to practice as a bonus on top</strong>.</p>
</blockquote>

<div class="results-box">
<h3>What changed along the way</h3>
<ul>
<li>The product name changed from <strong>MatchBook</strong> to <strong>MatchMind</strong> after research turned up a naming collision with an existing sports-betting brand.</li>
<li>The key benefit shifted from "walk on with a tactical plan" to <strong>"effortless capture + instant recall"</strong> — interviews showed that was the pain players actually felt, not the tactical-advice angle.</li>
<li>AI tips didn't disappear — they got demoted from the headline benefit to a <strong>bonus</strong> layered on top of the core journaling/recall experience.</li>
</ul>
</div>
