# 🎾 Never forget how you beat them last time.

**MatchMind** is a tennis match journal and opponent-scouting app for junior competitive tennis players — the kind who play USTA tournaments, face the same opponents over and over across a season, and are told by a parent or coach to "keep a journal" but never actually do it, because writing one by hand after a match is the last thing you want to do.

MatchMind's whole idea is to make logging a match take seconds instead of feeling like homework — score, a couple of quick scouting notes (typed or just spoken out loud), done. In exchange, it remembers everything for you: search any opponent you've played before and instantly see your head-to-head record, their playstyle, and what worked last time — plus an AI-generated tip on how to beat them and what to practice, as a bonus on top.

## The short version

- **Who it's for:** junior competitive USTA players, roughly ages 12–18
- **The core problem:** paper journals get pushed by parents and abandoned because writing one right after a match is effortful and unappealing
- **The fix:** effortless capture (type or dictate) + instant opponent recall, with AI practice tips as a bonus
- **Privacy:** every player's data is private and personal — nothing shared, nothing public. Under-13 accounts involve a parent, per COPPA.
