## What this step was

Before assuming MatchMind needed to be invented from scratch, the process looked at what already exists — other tennis apps that log matches, track opponents, or give coaching feedback. The goal was to find out whether this problem had already been solved well by someone else, and if not, exactly what gap was left open.

## Result

A handful of adjacent products turned up, each solving a piece of the puzzle but not the whole thing:

<div class="results-box">
<h3>What's out there</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Opponote</strong> — the closest direct competitor. Manual match logging + opponent notes, simple and private. Gap: every note is typed by hand, no AI, no effortless capture.</li>
<li><strong>TennisKeeper</strong> — logs scores and head-to-head records with long-term trend stats. Gap: built for stat-tracking, not fast journaling or opponent game-plans.</li>
<li><strong>SwingVision</strong> — AI-powered auto-scoring and shot stats from video. Gap: it's about your own shots, not opponent recall, and requires filming the match.</li>
<li><strong>TennisAI / OnCourtAI</strong> — AI coaching feedback on your own stroke technique. Gap: technique only, nothing about opponents or journaling.</li>
<li>A long tail of small manual match-loggers (Deucey, Tennis Alley, Tennisphere, and others) — evidence that a lot of people want to log matches, but none of them had solved effortless capture + recall + tips together.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="callout accent">
<h3>The key finding</h3>
<p>None of the players in Joshua's own tennis section actually use <em>any</em> of these apps. So the real competition for MatchMind isn't Opponote or TennisKeeper — it's <strong>doing nothing and relying on memory, or a paper journal that gets abandoned</strong>. That reframed the whole bar for success: MatchMind just has to be easier than a notebook and more reliable than memory, not "better than the other apps."</p>
</div>

This finding is why the positioning statement's competitive-alternative clause names memory, word-of-mouth, and paper notes — not other apps. See <a href="{{ '/positioning-statement.html' | relative_url }}">Positioning Statement</a>.
